# Misconception-Driven Learning
## What it is
Deliberately surfacing and addressing common misconceptions rather than just teaching correct information. Students who have their misconceptions challenged learn more deeply.
## How to apply
- Design quiz distractors based on known misconceptions
- When a student selects a misconception, provide targeted explanation
- Pre-test to identify existing misconceptions before teaching
- Use cognitive conflict: present scenarios where the misconception fails
## Examples of common misconceptions
- "ML models understand language" (they process statistical patterns)
- "More data always means better models" (quality matters more than quantity)
- "Neural networks work like the brain" (very loose analogy at best)

# Active Learning Pedagogy
## Principles
- Learners construct knowledge through engagement, not passive consumption
- Ask questions before providing answers
- Use concrete examples and analogies before abstract theory
- Practice with feedback is more effective than reading
## Techniques
- **Retrieval Practice**: Quizzes and flashcards force recall
- **Elaboration**: Ask "why" and "how" to deepen understanding
- **Interleaving**: Mix topics to strengthen discrimination
- **Concrete Examples**: Ground abstract concepts in real scenarios
- **Dual Coding**: Combine text with visuals/diagrams
## Common misconceptions
- Learning should feel easy (effortful retrieval is actually more effective)
- Re-reading is a good study technique (it's one of the least effective)
- Learning styles (visual/auditory) are scientifically unsupported

# Retrieval Practice
## What it is
The act of recalling information from memory — through testing, flashcards, or self-quizzing — strengthens memory more than re-reading or re-studying.
## Why it works
- Retrieval creates stronger memory traces than passive review
- Failed retrieval followed by feedback is especially powerful
- It identifies gaps that re-reading would miss
## Implementation
- Frequent low-stakes quizzes
- Flashcards with spaced repetition
- Practice problems before reading solutions
- Free recall: "write everything you remember about X"
## Key research finding
Students who practice retrieval retain 50% more than those who re-study the same material, even when study time is equal.

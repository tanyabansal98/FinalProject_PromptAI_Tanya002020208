# Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)
## What it is
The gap between what a learner can do independently and what they can do with guidance. Effective instruction targets this zone — not too easy (boring), not too hard (frustrating).
## Application in adaptive learning
- Track learner performance to estimate current level
- Present content slightly above demonstrated ability
- Provide scaffolding for tasks in the ZPD
- Remove scaffolding as competence grows
## Indicators
- Student scores 70-85% → in the ZPD (challenging but achievable)
- Scores > 90% → material too easy, increase difficulty
- Scores < 50% → material too hard, add prerequisites/scaffolding

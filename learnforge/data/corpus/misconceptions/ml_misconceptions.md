# Machine Learning Misconceptions
## ML models understand language/images
False. They process statistical patterns. GPT doesn't understand meaning — it predicts likely next tokens based on training data.
## More data always improves models
Not necessarily. Noisy, biased, or irrelevant data hurts performance. 1000 clean labeled examples often beat 100,000 noisy ones.
## Deep learning is always better
For tabular data, gradient boosting (XGBoost, LightGBM) often outperforms deep learning. Deep learning shines with images, text, and audio.
## Neural networks work like the brain
Very loose analogy. Biological neurons are vastly more complex. This metaphor can mislead about capabilities and limitations.
